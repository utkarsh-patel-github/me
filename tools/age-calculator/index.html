How to Use the Age Calculator

Using the Age Calculator is quick and simple:

  1. Enter Your Birth Date - Select the date, month, and year you were born using the date picker.
  2. Select End Date - By default, today's date is used. You can uncheck "Use Today" and select a different date if you want to calculate your age at a specific point in time.
  3. Click Calculate - Press the "Calculate Age" button to see your results.
  4. View Results - Your age will be displayed in years, months, and days, along with additional measurements.
  5. Share or Copy - Use the buttons at the bottom to share your results or copy them to clipboard.

The calculator accounts for leap years and varying month lengths to give you the most accurate calculation possible.

How We Calculate Your Age

Our age calculator uses a precise algorithm that accounts for the following:

  • Leap Years - We properly account for leap years (including the special rules for century years).
  • Month Lengths - Different months have different numbers of days, which our calculator handles correctly.
  • Date Order - If the end date is before the birth date, we'll notify you of the error.

The calculation is performed using the following steps:

  1. Calculate the difference in years between the two dates.
  2. Check if the birth month/day has occurred this year. If not, subtract 1 from the year difference.
  3. Calculate the month difference, accounting for if the birth day has occurred in the current month.
  4. Calculate the remaining days.
  5. Convert to other units (total months, days, hours, etc.) as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this age calculator?
Our age calculator is extremely accurate. It takes into account leap years, varying month lengths, and other calendar irregularities to provide the most precise results possible.
Can I calculate age for any historical date?
Yes, the calculator works for any valid dates in the Gregorian calendar. You can calculate the age between any two dates, past or future.
How does it handle leap years?
The calculator properly accounts for leap years, including the special rules for century years (e.g., 1900 is not a leap year, but 2000 is). If you were born on February 29, it correctly calculates your age on non-leap years.
Is my data private when using this calculator?
Yes, absolutely. All calculations are performed directly in your browser. We don't store, transmit, or share any of the dates or information you enter.
Can I use this for legal documents?
While our calculator is highly accurate, for official or legal purposes, we recommend verifying the results with an official source or legal authority if needed.
