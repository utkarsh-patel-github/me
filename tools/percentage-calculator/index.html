Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate what percent one number is of another?

To calculate what percent one number is of another, divide the first number by the second number, then multiply by 100. For example, to find what percent 25 is of 200: (25 ÷ 200) × 100 = 12.5%.

How do I calculate a percentage of a number?

To calculate a percentage of a number, convert the percentage to a decimal by dividing by 100, then multiply by the number. For example, to find 15% of 200: (15 ÷ 100) × 200 = 0.15 × 200 = 30.

How do I calculate percentage increase or decrease?

To calculate percentage change, subtract the original value from the new value, divide by the original value, then multiply by 100. If the result is positive, it's an increase; if negative, it's a decrease. For example, to find the percentage change from 100 to 120: ((120 - 100) ÷ 100) × 100 = 20% increase.

What is a good tip percentage?

Tipping customs vary by country and service type, but in many places, 15-20% is standard for good service at restaurants. For exceptional service, 20-25% may be appropriate. In the U.S., 15% is typically considered the minimum for standard service, 18% for good service, and 20%+ for excellent service.
